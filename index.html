Biography

Hello World! I am passionate about neurodegenerative diseases. I am a skilled computational neuroscientist and recently completed my PhD on the L-dopa response in Parkinson's disease. I analyse data of large longitudinal study cohorts with respect to clinical, genetic, and brain imaging. I am a quick learner and a team worker that gets the job done.

My main interest is the discovery of biomarkers for disease progression and refined diagnosis.

    Publications

    2021. Defining impaired olfaction in Parkinson’s disease: assessing agreement between four published methods.

    2020. The L-dopa response varies in pathologically confirmed Parkinson’s disease: a systematic review.

    Pitz V, Malek N, Tobias ES, Grosset KA, Gentleman S, Grosset DG; Mov Disord Clin Pract; doi: 10.1002/mdc3.12885.

    A good response to levodopa is a key feature of Parkinson's disease (PD), and a poor response suggests an alternative diagnosis, but the extent of variation in the levodopa response in definite PD is not well defined. This is a systematic review on pathologically confirmed Parkinson's and reported L-dopa responsiveness. In summary, the levodopa motor response varies in definite PD with 10% of definite Parkinson's being unresponsive. Explanations other than diagnostic inaccuracy should be explored.

    2019. L-Dopa responsiveness in early Parkinson’s disease is associated with the rate of motor progression.

    Malek N, Kanavou S, Lawton MA, Pitz V, Grosset KA, Bajaj N, Barker RA, Ben-Shlomo Y, Burn DJ, Foltynie T, Hardy J, Williams NM, Wood N, Morris HR, Grosset DG on behalf of the PRoBaND clinical consortium; Parkinsonism Relat Disorders; doi: 10.1016/j.parkreldis.2019.05.022.

    L-dopa responsiveness in Parkinson's disease (PD) varies, but the clinical correlates and significance of this are ill-defined. Patients were assessed before and after their usual morning L-dopa dose, using the MDS Unified PD Rating Scale Part 3 (MDS UPDRS 3), and rated as definite responders (≥24.5% improvement) or limited responders (< 24.5%). The main determinants of variation in the L-dopa response in early PD are age and motor severity. A limited L-dopa response is associated with faster motor progression.
